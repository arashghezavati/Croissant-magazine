The History Of The Croissant

What is the history of the croissant?

Most food historians trace the humble origins of the croissant to Austria, where a simple crescent-shaped pastry had been popular since the 13th century.

Who made the first croissant?

What country does croissant come from? The true version of who first made the croissant is disputed, with many variations in existence.

Is a croissant really French?

The kipferl is the ancestor of the modern-day croissant, so to answer the question of “where did croissants originate?” The answer is that croissants origin outside of France.

Moving from kipferl to croissant

By the 19th century, the kipferl was a popular pastry in France. This was long after Queen Antoinette had introduced this famous Austrian pastry to France.
